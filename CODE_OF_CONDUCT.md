# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

We, as contributors, maintainers, and community members of **Codebase Cartographer**, commit to making participation in this project respectful, professional, inclusive, and harassment-free for everyone.

This commitment applies regardless of age, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, sexual identity and orientation, socioeconomic status, technical background, or any other personal characteristic.

Technical disagreement is welcome. Personal attacks are not.

# English

## Expected Behavior

Community members are expected to:

- Communicate respectfully and professionally
- Focus criticism on the work, not the person
- Provide reproducible evidence where possible
- Respect different experience levels
- Help newcomers understand project conventions
- Use inclusive language
- Accept maintainer decisions after reasonable discussion
- Respect privacy and confidentiality
- Follow responsible security disclosure
- Keep discussions relevant to Codebase Cartographer
- Respect repository licensing and intellectual property

## Unacceptable Behavior

The following behavior is unacceptable:

- Harassment, intimidation, threats, or discrimination
- Insults, ridicule, personal attacks, or humiliation
- Sexualized language or unwanted sexual attention
- Trolling, sustained disruption, or deliberate provocation
- Publishing private information without permission
- Doxxing or impersonation
- Spam or unrelated advertising
- Fabricated test results or misleading issue reports
- Malicious code submissions
- Hidden telemetry, credential collection, or data exfiltration
- Malware or harmful attachments
- Public disclosure of an unpatched vulnerability without coordination
- Sharing private source code, passwords, keys, or databases
- Abuse of mentions, reviews, or reactions
- Circumventing moderation actions
- Retaliation against good-faith reporters

## Technical Disagreement

Participants should:

1. Explain the problem clearly.
2. Separate facts from preferences.
3. Provide evidence where possible.
4. Consider security, privacy, maintainability, compatibility, performance, and user impact.
5. Avoid assuming bad intent.
6. Accept final maintainer decisions.
7. Move extended debates to a dedicated issue or discussion.

A rejected contribution is not a judgment of the contributor as a person.

## Project Spaces

This Code of Conduct applies to:

- Issues
- Pull requests
- Code reviews
- GitHub Discussions
- Wiki pages
- Project boards
- Commit messages
- Release discussions
- Project-related email
- Official project chat rooms
- Public or private communication made in an official project role

## Maintainer Responsibilities

Maintainers may:

- Clarify acceptable behavior
- Remove harmful content
- Edit or reject inappropriate contributions
- Lock discussions
- Issue warnings
- Restrict participation
- Block accounts
- Protect reporters from retaliation
- Preserve confidentiality
- Escalate credible threats or illegal activity

Maintainers should apply consequences consistently and proportionately.

## Reporting

Report conduct concerns privately to the project owner or maintainers.

A useful report includes:

- Your contact information
- The account involved
- A description of the incident
- Dates and links
- Screenshots or references
- Whether the behavior is ongoing
- Whether there is an immediate safety concern
- Your preferred resolution

Do not publicly expose or confront the reported person on behalf of the project.

## Privacy and Confidentiality

Reports will be handled as confidentially as reasonably possible.

Information may be shared only with people who need it to evaluate or resolve the matter.

Absolute confidentiality cannot be guaranteed when legal duties, platform escalation, immediate safety risks, or a fair response require limited disclosure.

## Enforcement Guidelines

### 1. Informal Correction

Used for minor unprofessional behavior.

### 2. Formal Warning

Used for clear or repeated violations.

### 3. Temporary Restriction

Used for sustained disruption, hostility, or disregard of warnings.

### 4. Permanent Ban

Used for harassment, threats, doxxing, malicious code, retaliation, or repeated serious violations.

### 5. Immediate Escalation

Credible threats, malware, credential theft, or illegal content may be reported to GitHub, service providers, or authorities.

Maintainers do not need to apply these steps sequentially.

## Appeals

A person affected by enforcement may submit one concise appeal containing:

- The decision being appealed
- Relevant new information
- A reason for reconsideration
- Confirmation that future participation will follow this Code of Conduct

Appeals that only repeat previous arguments may be denied.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct governs behavior, not technical merit.

Technical contributions remain subject to [CONTRIBUTING](CONTRIBUTING.md) repository licensing, security requirements, project scope, and maintainer review.
